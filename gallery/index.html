Gallery

A longer look.

More of the house, for those who would like to see it. The order follows the day — arrival, the ground that gathers, the garden, the western evening, the quiet upstairs, and the materials close to hand. Photographs in time.

I · Arrival

The approach.

The road off the coast, the threshold, the first quiet inside. A single, soft nod to the faros that names the shore — no more than that.

II · The ground that gathers

Where the house opens.

The ground level is for gathering — living and dining open to the terrace, the pool, and the sea beyond. Reduced, unhurried lines.

III · The garden and terrace

The western frame.

Garden and terrace hold the horizon — old olives and cypress verticals, warm-sand shade sails for the midday, the evening kept for the sun.

IV · The western evening

The house’s moment.

The coast faces west. Each evening the sun lowers into the sea, and the stone holds the warmth it leaves behind.

V · The withdraw

Upstairs, turned down.

The upper floor withdraws for rest — the day’s warmth drawn inward, the rooms quieted and dimmed.

VI · Materials, close to hand

The palette, in detail.

The colours gathered from the place — the light of the sea, the cliffs, the coastal herbs, the inland woods — in the materials that carry them.

This is the house, in the order of its day. To know the rest is to be there — by invitation, in time.

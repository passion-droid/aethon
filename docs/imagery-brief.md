# AETHON — imagery brief for the photography / film team

**Rev B · July 2026 — synced to the live website.**

*A working brief: what the project is, the intention behind the pictures, and the exact
list of assets to create. Please read §1–§5 before shooting; §6–§8 are the production
detail and the shot list; Appendix A maps every placeholder frame on the live site.*

---

## 1. The project, in brief

**AETHON** is a single private seafront residence on the **western coast of Paphos, Cyprus**
(the *Faros* / lighthouse coast). It is a **quiet renovation** — a refinement, not a new
build — of an existing house (originally one of the *Faros Beach Houses*): built 2018,
renewed/completed 2026. The original architecture is by Vardastudio; the renewal keeps the
geometry and refines the light, materials and landscape.

The website is **not** an estate-agent listing. It is an evergreen, museal brand presence —
closer to an **architectural monograph** than a property page. Tone references: **Aman,
Tadao Ando.** The pictures must carry that register: calm, restrained, unhurried, no
real-estate gloss.

The name AETHON (Greek *Αἴθων*, "the blazing one") and the whole story are built around one
fact: **the coast faces west, so the evening — the last warm light of the day — is the
house's moment.**

## 2. The website is live — use it as the storyboard

The site these pictures are for is already built and public: **https://aethon.house**.
Every photograph's place exists today as a labelled placeholder frame — the caption under
each empty frame is the working title of the picture that belongs there. Two things to do
before shooting:

- **Walk the page once in each light.** The ☀ / ☾ control in the top bar switches the whole
  site between **Daylight** and **Afterglow** — the two moods the matched pairs (§3) will

There is also a deeper photo-essay at **aethon.house/gallery/** — six chapters,

## 3. The core idea these images must serve: **Daylight ↔ Afterglow**

The site has a **Daylight / Afterglow toggle**. It is not a dark mode and not a filter — it
is meant to reveal the **two characters of the same house**:

- **Daylight** — open, airy, outward to sea and sky; materials, horizon, indoor–outdoor living.
- **Afterglow** — the warm hour after sunset: intimate, sheltered, glowing; architectural
  lighting, reflections, the house lit from within.

> Day = *the house in the landscape.* Afterglow = *the landscape gathered around the house.*

**This is the single most important production instruction:** the key viewpoints must be
shot as **matched pairs — the same camera position, height, focal length and framing,
once in daylight and once in the afterglow** — so the site can crossfade between them on the
exact same composition. **Lock the tripod, shoot the day frame, return for the evening frame
without moving.** A pair that doesn't register precisely is unusable for the toggle.

---

## 4. Visual direction

- **The hero is the sunset, never the lighthouse.** No lighthouse, no beam, no nautical
  cliché. The "faros" is only a place name.
- **Afterglow = warm, not blue.** Shoot the evening set in the **warm window from sunset into
  early dusk** — golden, amber, the architectural lighting reading at roughly **3000 K**
  (the real scheme is warm, indirect, dimmable). **Avoid cool "blue hour."** This is a hard
  brand rule: the site has **no blue accent** anywhere — "the sea is evoked through light and
  stone, never blue." A cold blue dusk frame is off-brand.
- **Palette to honour** (these are the house's real materials — let them lead the colour):
  marble / travertine / coastal sandstone (warm stones), dark oak & iroko (warm woods),
  and the **single accent: green** — olive-green leather and a softer rosemary green. Warm
  neutrals + green + the warm light. Outdoor shade sails are **warm sand / oyster-white,
  never stark white.**
- **Celebrate the signature spatial move — the threshold dissolves:** the ground floor's
  glazed walls slide fully back so the **living and entertaining rooms, the ~10 m pool (laid
  against the house, with a shallow sun-shelf), the garden and the sea read as one space.**
  Frame this continuity wherever possible (open glazing, the eye carried from inside → pool →
  garden → western horizon). There is also a **planted internal courtyard** (garden brought
  inside) worth capturing.
- **Mood over inventory.** Reduction, negative space, stillness. Comfort is felt, not
  demonstrated — **no gadgetry**: no visible tech, screens, switches, thermostats, speakers,
  CCTV, solar panels. No "feature" shots.
- **People:** keep it about architecture and light. No identifiable people or owners; at most
  a distant, anonymous, atmospheric presence — and only if it adds calm, not lifestyle.

---

## 5. Hard guardrails (please respect — privacy & brand)

**Do**

- Lead with light, stone, water, green, horizon, the western evening.
- Leave calm negative space (the site overlays text on the hero — see §6).
- Keep everything warm-neutral and quiet.

**Don't**

- ❌ **No exact address, street signs, house numbers, neighbours' houses, or anything that
  pinpoints the location.** This is a private home.
- ❌ **Strip GPS / EXIF location from every delivered file** (or confirm we may). Do not post
  the originals anywhere public. (The site itself serves only metadata-stripped, downsized
  copies.)
- ❌ No blue / cold colour grade; no blue-hour as the evening look (use warm afterglow).
- ❌ No lighthouse, no beam, no nautical motifs.
- ❌ No real-estate conventions: no wide-angle distortion, no HDR "flare," no twilight
  composite look, no staged "lifestyle," no superlatives in captions.
- ❌ No visible technology, security, or solar hardware.

## 6. Technical specification

**Matched-pair method (the priority):** for every Tier-1 viewpoint, deliver a **`-day`** and
an **`-evening`** frame from an identical locked-off setup. Same lens, same height, same
framing. The site stacks them and crossfades.

**Timing of the evening set:** from a few minutes before sunset through the warm afterglow
(~20–40 min after). Stop before the sky goes cold/blue.

**Format & resolution:** deliver **high-resolution masters** (long edge **≥ 2400 px**, larger
welcome), **sRGB**, minimal/neutral grading. The website runs its own optimisation pipeline
(modern formats, responsive sizes, metadata stripped) — we need clean masters, not web
exports.

**Aspect ratios by slot** (compose with room to crop a little; Appendix A gives the
frame-by-frame list):

| Slot | Ratio | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Hero | landscape ~**3:2 / 16:9**, croppable | a **calmer area on the left** for the headline; light/interest to the **right**; must also survive a **tall phone crop** |
| Social / link-preview master | crops to **1200 × 630** (≈1.91:1) | one frame — warm afterglow register — that stays legible at thumbnail size; likely the hero-evening or west-elevation-evening frame |
| Establishing film plate (*The place*) | **21:9** | wide, situating; stills or a slow film |
| West elevation | **21:9**, shoot loose | the signature exterior; also used as a **full-height page-break plate**, so keep generous sky/foreground |
| Standard view frames (living · pool · terrace · interior…) | **16:10** | |
| Portrait frames (the olives · the lit olive) | **4:5** | |
| Material detail | **3:1** band | close texture (stone · olive leather · iroko) |
| Gallery feature plates | **21:9** | one per chapter, full-bleed |
| Gallery standard / portrait | **3:2 / 4:5** | — |

**Already done — do NOT reshoot:** the **8 material macro swatches**, the **two floor-plan
renders**, and the **concept sketch** (see Tier 4) are finished and wired. Materials:
*marble, patiti (hand-pressed lime plaster), travertine, coastal sandstone, olive-green
leather, rosemary green, dark oak, iroko.*

## 7. The shot list

Pairs marked **[D+E]** need both a daylight and an afterglow frame from the *same* setup.
Single-state shots note which state. Captions in quotes are the live placeholder labels —
the working titles.

### Tier 1 — the toggle's heart (do these first)
1. **Hero** — the establishing portrait of the house to the western sea. **[D+E]**
   *Day:* bright façade, open glazing, sea/sky. *Evening:* the façade lit warm from within
   against the afterglow. The evening frame is also the likely **social master** (§6).
2. **West elevation** (21:9, loose). **[D+E]** One locked-off pair serves three slots at
   different crops: the Views frame *"West elevation · sunset"*, the full-bleed page-break
   *"The house, toward the western sea"*, and the gallery's evening-chapter feature.
3. **Living, toward the sea** (16:10). **[D+E]** Glazing slid back; the eye carried inside →
   pool → garden → horizon. Also the gallery's chapter-II feature.
4. **The pool** (16:10). **[D+E]** *Day:* the sheet of water, sun-shelf, mirroring sky
   (*"The pool, mirroring the sky"*). *Evening:* *"The pool, at dusk"* — glowing softly at
   its edges, warm reflections.

### Tier 2 — section plates & the remaining view frames
1. **"Ancient olives, the dry garden"** (16:10). The garden section's plate — day-led;
   sculptural old olives, the dry planting.
2. **The olives, portrait** (4:5, two frames). *Day:* *"The garden, sculptural olives"*.
   *Evening:* *"The lit olive, after dark"* — a single old olive lit from below. A matched
   pair on one tree is welcome but two different compositions are fine.
3. **"The terrace, under shade sails"** (16:10). Day-led; the warm-sand sails (never stark
   white). An evening frame (*"The terrace, golden hour"*) is welcome for the gallery.
4. **The interior / the withdraw** (16:10). **[D+E]** *Day:* natural light, a calm room to
   the sea. *Evening:* *"The interior, lit from within"* / *"Lit from within, at evening"* —
   a low warmth, intimate, the house keeping its own evening.
5. **"The afterglow — lit from within"** (full-bleed page-break). The house glowing at dusk,
   seen from outside — may fall out of the west-elevation evening frame, or be its own setup.
6. **The internal courtyard** (4:5 or 16:10). The garden brought inside — day-led.
7. **Material detail band** (3:1). Static; close texture in warm raking daylight.

### Tier 3 — the `/gallery/` photo-essay (six chapters; can follow later)
The chapters, as titled on the live page: **The approach** → **Where the house opens** →
**The western frame** (garden & terrace) → **The house's moment** (the afterglow showpiece)
→ **Upstairs, turned down** → **The palette, in detail**. Appendix A2 lists all
twenty-three captioned frames. Much of it falls out of Tiers 1–2 at different crops; the
gallery-only setups are: the approach / the threshold / first light in the entrance,
dining at the long table, the kitchen in stone and oak, cypress verticals, the terrace at
golden hour, last light on the water, a room toward the sea, the stair rising, and a
bedroom turned down to a glow.

### Optional — motion (only if commissioned)
- **Hero ambient loop:** short, muted, *seamless* loop; minimal motion (sea light, stirring
  olive leaves). Will be gated behind reduced-motion and fall back to the still; the mobile
  hero stays a still by design.
- **Establishing film** (*"Establishing film · the western coast at sunset"*, 21:9): slow,
  deliberate, situating the house on the western coast at sunset. **Never aimless drone
  hovering.** A day and an evening version mirror the toggle.

### Tier 4 — artifacts (curated still-lifes, the "vitrine")

Treated like museum objects, not product shots: an isolated, tactile piece on a neutral
warm-stone ground, **raking / directional light to reveal texture**, shot as a **consistent
set** (same background, same light angle, same distance) so the series reads as one
collection. These are the materials of the house, in the hand — they fill the gallery's
sixth chapter (*The palette, in detail*).

| Artifact | Live caption | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Patiti (πατητή) lime plaster | "Patiti, pressed into waves" | a hand-pressed sample, the wave structure caught in side light |
| Stone + olive leather | "Travertine and olive leather" | the warm stone against the signature green leather (Nordic Grain) |
| Iroko & dark oak | "Iroko, close at hand" | an end-grain or a joinery edge of the real woodwork |
| Rosemary-green fabric | "Rosemary green, in fabric" | a single draped fold of the curtain / upholstery cloth |
| The set together | "The palette in the hand" | stone + leather + iroko arranged in one frame |
| Found objects | "Of the coast — rosemary, olive, stone" | a sprig of rosemary, an olive branch, a pale cliff pebble |

**Never** dimensioned drawings, spec sheets, or hardware that reads as a "feature."

**Already in hand — the concept sketch.** Cliff Tan's (Dear Modern) hand perspective is
wired on the live site in *The interior* — *"Concept sketch — the rooms, from within"* —
uncropped, toned into the site's palette. It is the model for this tier: an *authored,
atmospheric* artifact, never a technical drawing. Do not reshoot.

## 8. Delivery & hand-off

- Deliver **masters + RAW** via a private transfer (not a public link), and please don't
  post the frames anywhere public — the site publishes only metadata-stripped, downsized
  copies, and the house stays unlocatable.
- **Naming:** use the slot slugs with a state suffix, e.g.
  `hero-day` / `hero-evening`, `west-elevation-day` / `west-elevation-evening`,
  `living-sea-day` / `living-sea-evening`, `pool-day` / `pool-evening`,
  `interior-day` / `interior-evening`, `garden-olives`, `olive-lit`, `terrace`,
  `courtyard`, `material-detail`, `og` (the social master), and for the photo-essay
  `gallery-<chapter>-<subject>` (e.g. `gallery-arrival-threshold`).
- We run the optimisation pipeline and wire the pairs into the site's crossfade — clean
  masters are all we need.
- **Scale of a complete delivery:** the two pages hold roughly **25–30 unique setups**;
  with the matched pairs that is **~35 delivered selects**. Reuse across pages is expected —
  the same west-elevation pair serves the front page, the Views grid and the gallery at
  different crops.
- Questions / approvals: **mail@aethon.house**.

*Thank you — the whole site turns on the western light; these pictures are how a visitor
will finally understand why the house is called AETHON.*
